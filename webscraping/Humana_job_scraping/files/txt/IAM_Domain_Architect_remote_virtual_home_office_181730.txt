Humana is seeking a Domain Architect that will be responsible for the internal architecture design of the enterprise’s strategy for Identity and Access Management (IAM). This role is responsible for defining and documenting architectures using enterprise standards. The Domain Architect is also responsible for the effective integration of their domain into the broader business and IT ecosystem within which it will operate. The Domain Architect is not only called to understand and follow enterprise guidelines and design patterns in their work; they are also expected to share feedback and learning back out to the enterprise. Thus, the Domain Architect also serves as the SME for their domain to other architects, and as a feedback loop back into the enterprise architecture portfolio. Define current domain architecture, and identify issues within it. Refine and/or redesign domain architecture to meet the organization’s changing business requirements. Interact with other architects to make sure the domain aligns with the needs of the consumers. Interact with other Domain Architects to define domain interactions. Interact with consumers to promote and explain the domain and domain concepts. Serve as the “bridge” between Enterprise Information Protection and their related application architectures. Ensure domain architecture leverages enterprise capabilities wherever feasible. Ensure enterprise reference architecture standards, patterns, principles, and guides are documented. Ensure solutions are aligned with the enterprise reference architecture standards, patterns, principles, and guides. Define domain architectural and technical standards. Work with third-party solutions that affect the domain, including selection of technology (being sure it aligns with enterprise architecture). Treat the services within their domain as a portfolio of business assets. Review candidate services, and perform design-time governance on their domain’s services. Provide oversight to ensure implementations are consistent with defined architecture as well as consistent with business needs. Participate in the architecture design of the applications or technologies within the domain. Within their domain, recommend technologies that will increase cost effectiveness, user experience, and flexibility. Create and maintain domain architecture deliverables. Create consistency and collaboration throughout the domain across sub-area teams. Ensure appropriate lifecycle plans are created and implemented in compliance with required SOPs. Mentor and guide Technology Architects (App, Data, Tech) technically and professionally.
